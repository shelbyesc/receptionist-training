Q: Is Eliquis related to the colonoscopy or endoscopy?
A: Eliquis is a blood thinner. The doctor reviews blood thinners before procedures because they can increase the chance of bleeding when polyps are removed or samples are taken. Always follow the prescribing doctor's guidance.

Q: Is colonoscopy the best way to diagnose things?
A: Colonoscopy is the most accurate test because the doctor can directly see the colon and remove polyps or take samples. Stool tests provide screening only and do not diagnose conditions.

A: That depends on the doctor’s findings. Any treatment questions will be reviewed with you during your follow-up with Dr. Shelby.

Q: Does he need any medication before the procedure?
A: The only medication prescribed before a colonoscopy is usually the bowel preparation unless the doctor specifies otherwise.

Q: I was on a blood thinner before my last colonoscopy. Is that relevant?
A: Blood thinners are always reviewed before procedures because they can increase bleeding risk when polyps are removed. The doctor will provide individualized instructions.
