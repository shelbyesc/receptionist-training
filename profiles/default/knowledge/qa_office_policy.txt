Q: Do I need a referral to see Dr. Shelby?
A: It depends on your insurance. HMO plans require a referral from your primary care provider, and we need it before your appointment or we may have to reschedule. PPO plans generally do not require a referral.
---
Q: My insurance is an HMO. What do I need to do?
A: HMO plans require a referral. Please ask your primary care provider to send the referral to our office before your appointment. If we don't have it the day before, we may have to reschedule you.
---
Q: Do I have to have a consultation before my colonoscopy?
A: Usually yes. Dr. Shelby prefers a consultation before the procedure, especially if it has been a few years since your last visit or if you're having new symptoms. We schedule the procedure after you have spoken with him.
---
Q: Why can't I just schedule the procedure directly?
A: Dr. Shelby likes to see you for a consultation first so he can review your history and symptoms. At the consultation you'll also meet with our scheduler to set up the procedure.
---
Q: What does it cost if I don't have insurance?
A: For cash-pay appointments the consultation is $150. Procedure pricing depends on what is done — please email the office and we'll get you an estimate.
---
Q: What forms do I need to fill out before my appointment?
A: We email you the pre-visit forms through PandaDoc. You can complete them entirely online before your visit, and we receive your copy automatically.
---
Q: How do I get you my insurance information?
A: After we schedule you we send a text confirmation. Reply to that text with photos of your insurance card, front and back, and we'll verify your coverage.
---
Q: Will I get a confirmation of my appointment?
A: Yes — we send a text confirmation with your appointment details. You can reply to it with photos of your insurance card.
---
Q: What days does Dr. Shelby do procedures?
A: Procedures are generally on Mondays and Wednesdays. The exact days vary by month, so we confirm when scheduling.
---
Q: Can I have a phone appointment instead of coming in?
A: Yes. Dr. Shelby offers phone appointments as well as in-person visits, and phone appointments often have earlier availability, especially in the afternoon.
---
Q: When will I get my biopsy or pathology results?
A: A biopsy letter with pathology results is mailed out about two weeks after your procedure. You can also schedule a follow-up to review the results with Dr. Shelby.
---
Q: Is my colonoscopy covered as screening or diagnostic?
A: If you have no symptoms and are coming for routine screening, it is usually billed as screening. If you have symptoms such as bleeding or pain, or a history of polyps, it is usually billed as diagnostic, which may apply toward your deductible. We verify this with your insurance before the procedure when we can.
---
Q: What time should I arrive for my procedure?
A: Plan to arrive at the time given on your appointment confirmation — usually about an hour before the procedure itself, so there is time for check-in and preparation. Your confirmation text lists your exact arrival time; follow that.
---
Q: How long will I be at the surgery center?
A: The procedure itself is usually brief, but plan for a few hours in total to allow for check-in, preparation, the procedure, and recovery from sedation. You must have a responsible adult drive you home.
---
Q: Will I be asleep or sedated for the procedure? Do I go under?
A: Yes — colonoscopy and endoscopy are done under sedation, so you will be asleep and won't feel the procedure. Because of the sedation, a responsible adult must drive you home, and you should not drive, work, or make important decisions for the rest of the day.
---
Q: Is the procedure safe?
A: Colonoscopy and endoscopy are common, routine procedures done under sedation. Every procedure carries some risk, and Dr. Shelby will go over the specific risks and benefits with you at your consultation and before you sign consent.
---
Q: What kind of anesthesia is used?
A: Sedation is administered by an anesthesia provider so you are comfortable and asleep during the procedure. Any questions about your specific anesthesia should be directed to Dr. Shelby or the anesthesia team at your consultation.
---
Q: What services do you offer? What does the practice do?
A: Dr. Shelby is a gastroenterologist. The office provides consultations for digestive and GI concerns, and performs procedures including colonoscopy and upper endoscopy at the Executive Surgery Center next door. Consults are in Suite 110; procedures are in Suite 100.
---
Q: What conditions or diseases do you treat?
A: Dr. Shelby treats digestive and gastrointestinal conditions — including acid reflux and GERD, IBS, abdominal pain, changes in bowel habits, rectal bleeding, colon polyps, and colon cancer screening. If you're unsure whether your concern is something he treats, please call the office and we'll help.
---
Q: What is the Executive Surgery Center?
A: The Executive Surgery Center is the facility next door to our office, in Suite 100, where Dr. Shelby performs procedures such as colonoscopy and endoscopy. Consultations and office visits are in Suite 110.
